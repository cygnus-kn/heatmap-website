Today, I rearrange and add tit bits for my Notion dashboard and it has turned into something usable. I may make it my default online tracking for a while. Although I am an advocate for doing everything and not having to track it, the act of track does have its utilities, for example, when I feel unproductive and generally terrible about myself, I can turn to Notion as a confirmation that I have done something, while if I were to not track anything, my perception of terrible myself would not allow me to remember things that I've done. One thing it also help is to estimate the workload for tasks that I want to do. For example, I sometimes want to do a lot of things without realizing it, when I thing of an improvement to do, I act as if it is the only thing that I need to do. Now that everything is written down, I came to realize the actual workload or hours needed to put in to finish all of it. This also helps my anxiety because before, when I did not accomplish enough, the blame usually go to my own capability, now that I've known the core issue lying in my greedy need to do too many thing, I can somewhat rest assured that it's not entirely my fault, it eases my conscious and serves as a reminder that I need to do less.
