I think Eliezer is getting at a different definition of "belief" than Lex, when he believes something, it has to have high chance of manifesting, happening, or else, what you call a "belief" is actually just "hope". 
You [believe]"hope" that you are alright.
You [believe]"hope" that your life will be okay. 
You [believe]"hope" that you'll get a high grade. 

You say "believe" but in truth, you mean "hope". This is dangerous because you also use "believe" to express your actually belief.

You believe that it will rain tomorrow. (because you saw the weather forecast)
You believe that 1 + 1 = 2. (because that is a proven mathematical truth)
You believe that you have fever. (because your temperature is unusually hot)

To conflate between belief and hope is to ignore all evidence, and when you ignore evidence, even hope can't save you.
