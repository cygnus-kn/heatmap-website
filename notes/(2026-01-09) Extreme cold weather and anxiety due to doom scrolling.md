Seems like extreme weather has become a common occurrence lately here in Vietnam. It's the coldest in 20 years in Ho Chi Minh city, with the temperature gone as low as 18 degree this morning. I woke up this morning and didn't want to leave my bed because it so fucking cold and it is forecasted to continue for several more days.

Another extreme weather  I saw last year was unusually high tide. If I remember correctly, around the beginning of November last year, the tide got to the highest level recorded in the last 20 years. According to the picture taken in Thanh Đa Ward, the river overflowed into people's home, which has never happened before.

It painted a bleak picture of the future, where people's value are maximally extracted through strict punishment law. And also the arrest of a blogger who I recently follow, who writing about law and the mismanagement of the code of law in Vietnam. Turned out, she wrote like this for over 7 years already. I took a break and went to the kit-flying ground to just shake up my mind. It was peaceful, every time I look at the sky, at the kites are that are floating, I wish I was them, un-tethered to the world, unrestrained, free. There are too much darkness in this world that the last bastion of peace and quiet is all I have left, at least it feels like it. I might have to get off the internet for a while.
