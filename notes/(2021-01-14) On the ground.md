**On the ground**

I lost the key to my motorbike. It’s somewhat essential to going to places, nevertheless it’s not a major hinder for day-to-day live. It forces me to walk instead of riding.

In recent days, I’ve been having a bite at a street stand in front of a junior high school where they sells noodles at rock bottom price. The experience of walking outside at rush hour seems surreal, everyone’s been busying going place while I don’t need to. I feel like on the ground, literally, and metaphorically. On the ground being that you see what people do with your own eyes, not through official or social media report. They might put forth headlines such as “Ho Chi Minh busy as ever after the pandemic.” or “Business are returning to normal near the end of the year.” You can feel that while eating street noodles while looking around. Your are on the ground where it happened. I can see sea food stands, fried snack stand, confectionery stand, the kind of business that only thrive in a community that have money to spare. I can hear people talk about trivial matters, daily things, what they watched, where they went, what they’d do in Tet holiday instead of daily cases, how their work (or lack thereof) has been, or people whom they know, or someone they know who knows that person, is dead or hospitalized because of the virus. Usually I’d go to a coffee if I were to go outside, or go to a social club. Being “outside” technically but not meaningfully, whatever happened it did outside of the building.

I like being on the ground, and after having the key to my motorbike back, I still want to be on the ground and would still find, if not excuses, reasons to seek different grounds and maybe, engage in them.
