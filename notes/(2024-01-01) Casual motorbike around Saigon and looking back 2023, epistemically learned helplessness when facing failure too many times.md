I've just been back from a casual motorbike trip around Saigon, going to places whose similarity I could describe as "nature". And I've been thinking about this year, what would be the theme of it specifically. There are several interesting ideas: year of physicality, year of money, year of health improvement. Any of which contains something I want to do. 
If I had to describe last year, it would be a productive first half and a depressing and wasteful second half. There are some achievements that I obtained namely:
- Getting an IELTS 8.0
- Transitioning into teaching IELTS
- Founding and organizing ACX meetup Vietnam
- Maintaining a group of friends that actually align with my mindset.
- Finishing from start to finish, an actual class
- Flying the first trip abroad to the Philippines 
- Participating in a really cool conference hosted by Effective Altruism
- Implement several tools for my knowledge management system such as Notion and Obsidian.
But there are also things that aren't as positive:
- Lose my gf of more than 2 yrs.
- Become more indulgent nearly at the end of the year (play more video games and pay too much for food).
- Less physically active despite my personal need for improvement of physical 
- My mind got less active and engaged, which is the result of over-indulgence.
There are uncertain things also:
- One thing on my mind is the looming prospect of marriage and the concerning apathetic march of time. The idea that I should hurriedly find a person of marriage quality and a person of matching mindset are of conflicting incentive. 
- The idea that I should make more money, which is a trade off of my free time - a thing I strive to get more of.
- The distracting nature of the modern life and its small habit gradually deteriorate my brain and how to effectively deal with it without sacrificing social life.
- The alarming rate of isolation on the level of society that leads to the evaporation of social places leaving only the most basic and braindead place to become a Schelling point for social contact. Thereby further isolates people of academically-excellent and deeply introspective. 
- The shrinking of affordable vendors which abundantly exist in the past as a cost-effective point of buying things. Now due to increase in property rent, forced to closed shop not just move elsewhere but permanently leave the economy. 
I guess what I am concerned about is the lack of "Slack" in a society that leads to hyper-competition, one which destroys value and leaves anyone participating in worse-off situation than previously. 

Solving my own problem seems trivia compared to societal issues that can't be overcome by even social effort. In epistemology, if a person fall into too many situations in which his wit cannot get him out of it, he will lose the ability to think at all. Which is a dangerous trap to fall into. The term is epistemically-learned helplessness. Similarly, if I think about societal problems that deeply affects myself, those which I can't actively solve, then I will succumb to the same helplessness and become pessimistic and passive. This is problematic because upon realizing this, the least-effort solution is to turn a blind eye to reality. Which goes against my own belief of rationality, of understanding reality as closely as possible. This then posits a philosophical conundrum: take off your belief or lose your sanity. Both of which are unfavorable options. 

This is at the core of my uncertainty, that my own mindset to interact with the world is destroying my own sanity, not today, not tomorrow, not even in 5 years, but it does, eventually. And it will come a point where I have to make a decision, to abandon my own belief or to live a happy live, which is a paradox itself. I wouldn't be truly happy to live in a pseudo-constructed world, because simply it isn't a world, it's an illusion of one.

This goes too deep than what I anticipated for this casual writing. It feels weird, because of this format, I worried that I would write for too long, as it wouldn't fit to the rest of my writing. But that fear is, as far as I am concern, isn't founded in anything reasonable. So I will keep writing.

Back to the more surface of my year and what I want in a year especially for the next one. I'd like to improve my health. I'd like to not playing video game anymore, at least not at the intensity that I am doing now. I'd like to feel sharp in my head again. And I'd like to expand the circle of my friends to other like-minded individuals. As the experiment of hosting ACX Meetup let me know. There are those who like me out there. I just have to find them, at all cost. 

For my own romantic relationship, yes, I'd like to find a new person who is compatible with me on an intelligence level, as well as on a traditional level. A traditional level is one thing that is difficult to explain. I guess their social circumstance shouldn't be too far from mine. Such as being too rich, or being too obsess with look, or working in a non-academic field.

For my work, I'd like to go straight to doing a class of IELTS, finish analyzing some IELTS materials and find more IELTS related teaching material to study.
